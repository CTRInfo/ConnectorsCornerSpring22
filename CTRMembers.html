New Faculty Spotlight

Muhammad Rahman, PhD

Muhammad Rahman is a Data Scientist Faculty in the Center for Translational Research at Children's National Research Institute. Before joining Children's National, he was an AI research fellow at National Institutes of Health. Prior to that, he was a postdoctoral researcher in Whiting School of Engineering at Johns Hopkins University. He received a Ph.D. degree in computer science from the University of Maryland, Baltimore County. He had a master's degree in information technology and bachelor’s degree in computer science with summa cum laude.

Dr. Rahman’s research focuses on artificial intelligence (AI), natural language processing, machine learning, data analytics and public health. He has more than 10 years of research and development experiences in these fields. He has published more than 20 peer-reviewed research articles in top computer science conferences and journals. He is a program committee member and reviewer of many top-ranked CS conferences and journals. Dr. Rahman is keen to investigating and developing new machine learning and AI techniques to understand child well-being by leveraging big data.
